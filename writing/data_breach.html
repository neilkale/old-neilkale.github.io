Data Breach at Genesis

2019

The movie Gattaca is set in a dystopian future where DNA means everything and test-tube babies with perfect genomes are the norm. The main character, a natural-born man with genetic imperfections, has raised the ire of the government for trying to work at the space agency, a privilege reserved for the genetically perfect. Unfortunately for our protagonist, the government has a roster of every citizen's genome. In a world of handheld retina scanners and fingerprint identity matchers, he must resort to wild tricks to avoid getting caught. While Gattaca is far from real, it makes some interesting points. In a world where people readily give up their DNA to companies in exchange for a report detailing their ancestry, one must wonder where all that DNA goes. It certainly isn't being destroyed, and in a world of evergrowing technology, what could it eventually be used for? The following is a satirical piece written by me and my fellow classmate about what might happen if people keep being so open about their DNA.

Dear Valued Customer,

If you are receiving this message, your DNA may have been gently
relocated in a slight network breach. We value your business, and ask
for you to bear with us during this trying time. There is no need for
alarm as DNA only lasts about 6.8 million years. You’ll be off the
records in no time. In the meantime, you may encounter a few minor
anomalies. If you come home to find a long-lost identical twin replacing
you, bear in mind that it may just be a clone. Never fear, there are
procedures in place to deal with this event. For a small fee, we’d be
happy to incinerate your clone(s) and dispose of any remains. Of course,
we would “never” make clones without your approval, just like we would
“never” leak your data.

If you are enrolled in the “Baby+” program, you may be concerned about
the status of your child’s genetic alterations. Here at Genesis, we love
your precious little angel’s DNA far more than you do. Of course, you
don’t know how much your sweetum’s DNA sells for on the black market. We
see that you’ve ordered a purple-skinned, green-haired, 7-legged babe
with two adorable little horns on his forehead. That’s very
fashion-forward of you; he’ll be all the rage in another 10 years. As
for soon-to-be parents who haven’t yet enrolled in “Baby+”, your child
is a blessing. Doesn’t he deserves the best opportunities in life? Don’t
let your old-fashioned sense of ethics and morality deprive your child
of a happy life. Society’s opinion is irrelevant, all that matters is
that your child is perfect to you, and here at Genesis, we’re committed
to supporting your needs.

Now, for the good news, we estimate that almost 2% of you won’t be
affected by a genetic attack based on the DNA we auctioned off. We’d
like to remind you that this procedure is fully legal and described in
Terms and Services that you mindlessly signed, with the hope of finding
your origins. Not to worry, this won’t affect our operations. Thanks to
your generous contributions, we have a few hundred billion samples of
DNA remaining in the US, which other countries are still bidding for.

Valued customer, we note that you’ve been with Genesis for over 10
years, since 2019. We would hate to lose your business due to this
unfortunate event. Of course, if you should choose to leave the program,
we still hold the full rights to your DNA, so there’s really no point.
Besides, your purchase of a genealogy kit has done far more than track
down your fifteenth cousins. With this data, you can find out if you’re
more likely to get addicted to coffee, and still get exploited for more
money. It’s a win-win. On the bright side, now that your genetic data is
out in the open, you don’t have to worry about keeping it a secret
anymore.


Here at Genesis, we are committed to human perfection. As the saying
goes, “There’s CRISPR for lispers, and Cas9 for redesigns.” Our company
envisions a future where people can be their best self. One where people
are born to soar across the sky. In fact, our work with bird genomes and
chimeras may soon make that a reality. Also, immortality is coming soon
- for those of you who can afford it.

We promise that we aren’t selling data directly to the foreign
government, only their "unaffiliated” companies. Don’t be mistaken,
we’re motivated by greed, not politics. We’ll be more careless with the
future of humanity if you trust us this time. But don’t worry, we know
you will.

For further information and assistance, please contact our customer
service at 1-800-GENESIS.

Sincerely,


The Genesis Corporation
